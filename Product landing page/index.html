"Kairo" - so how it works?

To put it simply, Kairo are warming bags which main purpose is to warm up your body. Outside of Japan they are referred to as "hand warmers", but the difference with kairo is not only the finest quality, but also the fact that they serve to warm not only your hands. We also sell attachable kairo which you can use for different parts of the body. You can actually stick them to your clothes - you use when you are on the move and feel warm at all times. The warmth can least as long as 24 hours.
We are selling two kinds of Kairo - nonadhesive and attachable.

・non-adhesive Kairo
you can use them for your hands - keep them in your coat pocket or hold them in your hands - perfect for cold days.

・adhesive Kairo
You can attach them to almost every part of your body.


Now, take a closer look at the non-adhesive kairo types we are selling.

・NECK

Warming the neck will warm the whole body efficiently since that is where thick blood vessels are located. There is an acupuncture point that warms the whole body, called "Daishi".

・STOMACH

When the whole body cools down, it is effective to heat the point called "Kikai" which is two fingers below the navel. It is said to be a magical acupoint and warming it up will make you feel well.

・BACK

There is a large muscle called the trapezius from the shoulder to the back, but when it cools down, blood flow deteriorates and the muscle becomes hard. In such case, it is recommended to warm up your body between the shoulder blades.

・HIP

Hip is a place that gets cold easily. Behind the navel, there is an acupoint called "Meimon" and two fingers outside it is called "Jinyu". You can warm your entire hip by applying kairo to this area.

・FEET

When it comes to feet, it is especially the fingertips that you should warm up. In order to warm up the whole body it is recommended to stick kairo to your ankle. Behind the inner ankle and between the Achilles tendon, there is also a point called "Taikei" which is also said to be getting cold quick.
